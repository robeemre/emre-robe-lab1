Public Transport

Public transit in Toronto and its suburbs is generally provided by the Toronto Transit Commission (TTC) and is the busiest transit system in Canada. Mainline service is provided by Lines 1 and 2, which offer north-south and east-west subway service between the downtown core and inner suburbs. Several major streets in the city are serviced by streetcar lines, some of which operate in dedicated tracks separated from other traffic. Widespread bus service provides connections to other areas not serviced by rail. Together, these mass transit services served over 3 million daily trips (pre-Covid).
